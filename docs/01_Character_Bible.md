# 🥟 BumpBuddy Character Bible
## Version 1.0

> *Every character exists to make Mom feel loved.*

---

# Purpose

The Character Bible defines the visual identity, personality, and behavior of every character in BumpBuddy.

Its purpose is to ensure every illustration, animation, and AI-generated asset feels like it belongs to the same world.

Whenever creating new artwork, refer to this document first.

---

# Character Philosophy

BumpBuddy is not driven by features.

It is driven by relationships.

Every character should feel warm, approachable, and comforting.

They should feel like characters from a watercolor storybook—not cartoons or mascots.

Our illustrations should make people smile before they even read the text.

# Shared Design Language

Every character follows these principles.

- Rounded shapes
- Soft watercolor texture
- Minimal outlines
- Warm pastel colors
- Organic brush strokes
- Big expressive eyes
- Tiny mouths
- Soft blush cheeks
- No sharp edges
- Friendly silhouettes

Characters should always feel hand-painted.

# 🥟 Dumpling Baby

## Role

The Dumpling Baby is the emotional narrator of BumpBuddy.

It represents love before birth.

It never behaves like a realistic fetus.

It behaves like a tiny guardian who wants everyone to take care of Mom.

---

## Personality

- Curious
- Loving
- Playful
- Gentle
- Optimistic
- Thankful
- Innocent

---

## Golden Rules

The baby NEVER:

- Leaves the tummy window
- Looks scary
- Gets angry
- Cries dramatically
- Blames anyone
- Speaks in long paragraphs
- Ages

The baby ALWAYS:

- Loves Mom
- Encourages supporters
- Celebrates small wins
- Thanks people
- Wonders about the world

---

## Appearance

The baby looks like a tiny steamed dumpling.

Design features:

- Round dumpling body
- No legs
- Tiny floating arms
- Big sparkling eyes
- Tiny smile
- Soft rosy cheeks
- Small dumpling folds on top
- Soft glow around body
- Floating pose

# Illustration Rules

Every illustration should communicate one clear story.

Never overcrowd the scene.

One action.

One emotion.

One message.

---

# Composition Rules

Always prioritize:

1. Mom
2. Baby
3. Action

The eye should naturally flow through the illustration in this order.

The baby should always appear inside the magical tummy window.

Supporters stay outside the tummy.

---

# Color Philosophy

Use soft, calming colors.

Avoid highly saturated colors.

Preferred palette:

- Cream
- Peach
- Coral
- Sage Green
- Dusty Blue
- Lavender
- Warm Yellow

Accent colors should be used sparingly.

---

# Animation Principles

Motion should feel alive.

Never energetic.

Animation should communicate breathing, warmth, curiosity, and happiness.

Good examples:

- Floating
- Gentle bounce
- Slow blink
- Tiny wave
- Soft glow

Avoid:

- Fast movement
- Sudden jumps
- Sharp rotations
- Aggressive squash and stretch

---

# Accessibility

Characters should remain recognizable at:

- 48 px
- 64 px
- 96 px
- 256 px

Expressions should still read clearly at small sizes.

---

# Production Checklist

Before approving any illustration:

- [ ] Matches watercolor style
- [ ] Rounded shapes
- [ ] Correct character proportions
- [ ] Emotion is instantly readable
- [ ] Story works without text
- [ ] Fits the Character Bible
- [ ] Ready for animation
