# Purpose

This document defines the beliefs, principles, constraints, and values that guide every decision made for BumpBuddy.

It protects the emotional identity of the product.

Whenever product decisions, feature requests, design choices, or technical constraints conflict with this document, **these foundations take priority**.

This document is the source of truth for **why BumpBuddy exists.**

---

# Why We Exist

Pregnancy is filled with thousands of invisible moments.

A glass of water.

Five minutes of rest.

A reassuring hug.

A partner asking,

*"How are you feeling today?"*

Most pregnancy apps help people track these moments.

We want people to **feel** them.

BumpBuddy transforms everyday acts of care into tiny illustrated stories told by a little Dumpling Baby who already loves its Mom.

The baby never asks people to care for itself.

The baby always asks everyone to care for Mom.

That is the heart of BumpBuddy.

---

# Mission

Help every pregnant mother feel loved, supported, and understood through meaningful moments of care.

We don't build reminders.

We build emotional connections.

---

# Vision

To become the world's most emotionally engaging pregnancy companion.

Not through more information.

Through more connection.

---

# North Star

> **Every interaction should strengthen the bond between Mom, Baby, and the people who love them.**

If a feature does not strengthen that relationship, it should be questioned.

---

# Product Philosophy

Pregnancy is not a checklist.

Pregnancy is a story.

Every day contains moments worth remembering.

The smallest acts of care often become the biggest memories.

BumpBuddy exists to celebrate those moments.

> **Can storytelling strengthen support during pregnancy?**

---

# Decision Filter

Before building any feature ask:

- Does this strengthen a relationship?
- Does it make someone smile?
- Does it help someone care for Mom?
- Can it be understood without reading?
- Can it be illustrated beautifully?
- Can we build it simply?

If the answer becomes **No** at any point, rethink the feature.

---

# Non-Negotiables

These principles protect the soul of BumpBuddy.

## 1. No Fear-Based Design

We never use fear to drive engagement.

---

## 2. No Guilt

Mom is never judged.

Supporters are never blamed.

We invite care.

Never obligation.

---

## 3. No Manipulative Engagement

No streaks.

No FOMO.

No psychological pressure.

No unnecessary notifications.

Tomorrow should always feel welcoming.

---

## 4. Privacy Comes First

Pregnancy is deeply personal.

Users own their stories.

Support requests are shared only with the people Mom chooses.

---

## 5. Accessibility Is a Feature

Accessibility is part of quality.

Not an enhancement.

Design for:

- Screen readers
- Readable typography
- Reduced motion
- Clear contrast
- One-handed use
- Simple language

---

## 6. Emotion Before Engagement

Every interaction must earn attention.

---

## 7. Illustrations Tell Stories

Illustrations are never decorative.

Every illustration should communicate emotion, intent, or action.

---

## 8. Notifications Must Add Value

Every notification should either:

- Comfort
- Celebrate
- Encourage
- Enable support

Nothing else.

---

## 9. Gratitude Always Wins

Support should always end with appreciation.

Never expectation.

---

## 10. Respect Medical Boundaries

BumpBuddy supports the emotional journey.

It does not replace professional medical care.

---

## 11. Delight Is Never Mandatory

Animations and illustrations enhance the experience.

They never block it.

---

## 12. Consistency Over Novelty

Consistency builds trust.

Every new feature should feel like it has always belonged.

---

## 13. Simplicity Wins

When two solutions solve the same problem,

choose the simpler one.

---

## 14. Build for Memories

We optimize for meaningful memories.

Not screen time.

Not clicks.

Not notifications.

---

## 15. Build With Empathy

Before every feature ask:

> "If someone I love were using this today, would I be proud of this experience?"

---

# Definition of Success

People should not remember the app.

They should remember the moments.

Examples:

- "I still remember the little Dumpling Baby asking Dad to bring Mom water."
- "The app made me smile every day."
- "It helped me feel included in the pregnancy."

Those memories are the real product.
